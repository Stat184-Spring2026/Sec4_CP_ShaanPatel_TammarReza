# Project Plan

## Project Topic

Our project is about food price changes over time in the United States. We want to study how prices have changed for groceries, restaurant meals, and major food categories.

## Research Goal

The main goal of our project is to explore how food prices have changed over time and identify important trends in the data. We want to compare food at home, like groceries, with food away from home, like restaurant meals. We also want to look at food categories such as fruits, vegetables, meat, dairy, and bakery items to see which categories changed the most.

## Why We Chose This Topic

We chose this topic because food prices are something that affects everyday life and are easy for people to understand and relate to. This topic also gives us a good chance to explore real trends using reliable public data. We think it will help us create strong graphs, useful tables, and a clear final report.

## Main Questions We Want to Explore

- How have food prices changed over time in the United States?
- Have grocery prices or restaurant prices increased faster?
- Which food categories have changed the most?
- Are some food categories more stable than others?

## Data Sources We Plan to Use

We plan to use official public data sources about food prices in the United States. Our main sources include:

- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Consumer Price Index data
- FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)
- USDA Economic Research Service (ERS)

These sources provide real data on food prices over time, including food at home, food away from home, and major food categories.

## What We Need to Do

To complete this project, our team will need to:

- find and confirm good data sources
- make sure the data follow the project rules
- import and clean the data in R
- study the data and look for patterns
- create graphs and tables that clearly show trends
- explain where the data came from
- write about the FAIR and CARE principles
- create a final report in Quarto that explains our results clearly

## Planned Steps

1. Finalize our research questions.
2. Find and confirm the data sources we want to use.
3. Learn more about the data and where they came from.
4. Import the data into R.
5. Clean and organize the data.
6. Create summary tables and visualizations.
7. Compare grocery prices, restaurant prices, and major food categories.
8. Write up our findings in a clear and organized way.
9. Prepare our final report and presentation materials.
10. Revise our work based on feedback.

## Planned Visuals

We currently plan to include visuals such as:

- a line graph comparing food at home and food away from home over time
- a line graph showing changes in major food categories
- a bar chart showing which categories increased the most
- one or more tables that summarize important trends in the data

## Team Goals

As a team, we want to improve our skills in:

- making clear and professional visuals

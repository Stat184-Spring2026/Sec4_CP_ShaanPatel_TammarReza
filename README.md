# Food Price Changes Over Time in the United States

This repo contains our Stat 184 course project about how food prices in the United States have changed over time. We are focusing on grocery prices, restaurant prices, and major food categories.

## Overview

The goal of this project is to explore how food prices have changed over time and find important trends in the data. We want to compare food at home, like groceries, with food away from home, like restaurant meals. We also want to look at food categories such as fruits, vegetables, meat, dairy, and bakery items to see which ones changed the most.

Our team will use exploratory data analysis to study the data, make graphs and tables, and explain what we find in a clear way. We also want to improve our skills in finding reliable data, cleaning data in R, and creating a strong final report.

## Data Sources and Acknowledgements

We plan to use official public data sources about food prices in the United States. Our main sources include:

- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Consumer Price Index data
- FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)
- USDA Economic Research Service (ERS)

These sources give us real data about food prices over time, including grocery prices, restaurant prices, and different food categories. We will cite all of our data sources in the final report.

## Current Plan

Our plan is to study how food prices have changed over time in the United States. We want to compare grocery prices and restaurant prices, and also see which food categories have increased the most.

To do this, we will:
- find and confirm good data sources
- import and clean the data in R
- make graphs and tables to show trends
- compare categories and time periods
- explain where the data came from
- create a final report that clearly explains our results

More details about our project plan are included in our planning document in this repo.

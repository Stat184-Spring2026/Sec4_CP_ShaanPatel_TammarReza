# Food Price Changes Over Time in the United States

This repo contains our Stat 184 course project about how food prices in the United States have changed over time. We focus on grocery prices, restaurant prices, and major food categories.

## Overview

The goal of this project is to explore how food prices have changed over time and identify important trends in the data. We compare food at home, such as groceries, with food away from home, such as restaurant meals. We also look at food categories such as fruits and vegetables, meat and poultry, food and beverages, and nonalcoholic beverages to see which categories changed the most.

Our team used exploratory data analysis to study the data, create graphs and tables, and explain the main patterns we found. The project also helped us practice finding reliable data, cleaning data in R, creating visualizations, and writing a final report.

### Interesting Insight

One main insight from our project is that food prices increased over time, but not all categories increased at the same rate. In our analysis, meat and poultry had the largest percent increase among the categories we compared, while nonalcoholic beverages had the smallest percent increase.

## Data Sources and Acknowledgements

We used official public data sources about food prices in the United States. Our main sources include:

- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Consumer Price Index data
- FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)
- USDA Economic Research Service (ERS)

These sources provide real data about food prices over time, including grocery prices, restaurant prices, and different food categories. We cite these sources in the final report.
